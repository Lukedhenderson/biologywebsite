What is Blue Light?

Blue light refers to visible light in the 380–495 nm wavelength range. It is emitted naturally by the sun and artificially by LED screens. It has benefits like boosting alertness and mood but raises concerns when exposure is excessive or at night.

Vision Damage Myth

Stories claiming that normal screen use causes permanent eye damage are based on in vitro studies using unnaturally high blue light levels. Real-world exposure levels from devices do not cause retinal harm.

Eye Strain Myth

While 77% of adults report digital eye strain, evidence shows this is due to extended near-focus and reduced blinking, not blue light. Blue-blocking lenses have not proven effective at preventing eye fatigue.

Effects on Sleep

Blue light suppresses melatonin and significantly shifts circadian rhythms, disrupting sleep quality more than other wavelengths. Limiting screen use before bedtime is recommended.

Research Gaps

Long-term effects of blue light on vision remain uncertain due to the recent prevalence of high screen time. More studies are needed to evaluate mitigation strategies like blue-blocking glasses and night modes.

Conclusions

  • No evidence that usual screen use causes permanent eye damage or disease.
  • Digital eye strain stems from prolonged focus, not blue light.
  • Blue light meaningfully disrupts sleep; reduce nighttime exposure.
  • Further research is needed on long-term vision impacts and effective mitigation.
